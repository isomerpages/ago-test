<blockquote>
<h4><strong>AGO’s audit mandate is provided for in legislation. The key legislation that govern AGO’s work are the Constitution of the Republic of Singapore and the Audit Act.</strong></h4>
</blockquote>
<p>The Auditor-General has the duty to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Audit and report on accounts of all departments and offices of Government;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Audit such accounts of public authorities and bodies administering public
funds as prescribed by law; otherwise upon request and with approval of
Minister; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Recommend and comment in his report on all matters relating to public
accounts, public moneys and public stores.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The Auditor-General shall in his audit, ascertain whether reasonable steps
have been taken to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Safeguard the collection and custody of public moneys or other moneys
subject to his audit;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Ensure that payments have been made with proper authority and supported
by sufficient proof of payment; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Ensure that the provisions of law relating to moneys or stores have been
complied with.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>
<h4><strong>The Audit Act also provides for the Auditor-General to bring to the notice of the Ministry of Finance any serious irregularities that have occurred in the accounting or custody of public moneys or public stores.</strong></h4>
</blockquote>
<p>At the end of the audit, the Auditor-General will report on the Government
financial statements and his observations on internal controls and areas
where due regard is not given by the entities audited. This report is submitted
to the President, who shall then present it to Parliament.</p>
